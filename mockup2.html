Barack Obama has come under fire from within his party for giving in to Iranian demands over a nuclear deal that is expected as soon as tomorrow.

While John Kerry and Mohammad Javad Zarif, the Iranian foreign minister, continued negotiations last night in Lausanne, Switzerland, politicians at home warned the president that they would do all they could to scupper an agreement.

Robert Menendez, the leading Democrat on the Senate foreign relations committee, accused the president of wanting “a deal for a deal’s sake”, saying that Mr Obama was not simply “inching closer to Iran’s negotiating position but leaping toward it with both feet”.

The British foreign secretary was in Washington urging those in Congress not to undermine the negotiations as the end of March deadline approaches.
